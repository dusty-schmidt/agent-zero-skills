# The Professional's Code

## A Universal Operating System

The "Theory of Daily Fantasy Sports" is a masterclass in Rationality. It provides a structured, mathematical lens through which to view the world, applicable across all domains of decision-making under uncertainty.

## The Core Principles

### 1. Identify the Game Structure
**Question**: What type of ecosystem are you operating in?

- **Is it Zero-Sum, Negative-Sum, or Positive-Sum?**
- **What is the Rake?** (Transaction costs, fees, friction)
- **Who are your competitors?** (Professionals vs. recreational players)
- **What are the structural constraints?** (Regulations, market access, capital requirements)

### 2. Calculate Expected Value
**Focus**: Ignore the outcome. Focus on the decision quality.

```
∑(P × V) = Expected Value
```

**Process Over Results**: The quality of a decision is determined at the time it's made, not by the outcome.

**Key Questions**:
- What are the probabilities of each outcome?
- What are the values/payoffs for each outcome?
- How does this compare to alternatives?
- What new information could change this calculation?

### 3. Seek Leverage
**Question**: Where is the crowd wrong? Where is the "Yellow Jelly Bean"?

**Contrarianism Framework**:
- **High Leverage**: Low ownership + High probability of success
- **Negative Leverage**: High ownership + Low probability of success ("Bad Chalk")
- **Blue Ocean**: Create uncontested market space
- **Red Ocean**: Compete in existing space (high ownership)

### 4. Manage Structure
**Use Correlation** to engineer the volatility profile:

**Safety vs. Ceiling Trade-off**:
- **Cash Games/Conservative**: Minimize variance, protect geometric mean
- **GPPs/Aggressive**: Maximize variance, access fat tails
- **Barbell Strategy**: Combine extreme safety with extreme risk-taking

### 5. Protect the Bankroll
**Use the Kelly Criterion** to survive variance.

```
f* = (bp - q) / b
```

**Risk Management Principles**:
- **Ruin is the only enemy**: Never bet more than you can afford to lose
- **Position sizing**: Scale bets to edge and bankroll
- **Via negativa**: Often better to eliminate bad decisions than add good ones

### 6. Master Psychology
**Override Loss Aversion and Recency Bias** with systems and logic.

**Cognitive Bias Mitigation**:
- **Confirmation Bias**: Actively seek disconfirming evidence
- **Recency Bias**: Trust long-term baselines over recent outliers
- **Loss Aversion**: Reframe as sunk costs, focus on forward-looking decisions
- **Dunning-Kruger**: Radical accountability, attribute wins to variance

## The Professional's Decision Framework

### Pre-Decision Analysis
1. **Ecosystem Identification**
   - What type of game is this?
   - Who are the participants?
   - What are the structural constraints?

2. **Information Assessment**
   - What information do I have?
   - What information is missing?
   - How can I improve information quality?

3. **Value Calculation**
   - What are the possible outcomes?
   - What are the probabilities?
   - What are the values?

4. **Strategic Positioning**
   - Where is the crowd?
   - Where is the leverage?
   - How can I differentiate?

### Decision Execution
1. **Risk Management**
   - How much should I allocate?
   - What's my downside protection?
   - What's my exit strategy?

2. **Process Implementation**
   - Execute the decision systematically
   - Monitor for new information
   - Adjust as needed

### Post-Decision Review
1. **Outcome Evaluation**
   - What actually happened?
   - How did it compare to expectations?

2. **Process Assessment**
   - Was my decision-making sound?
   - What did I learn?
   - How can I improve?

3. **System Updates**
   - What assumptions were wrong?
   - How should I adjust my models?
   - What new strategies have I discovered?

## The Meta-Principles

### 1. Process Excellence
**Focus on decision quality, not outcomes.**
- Develop repeatable processes
- Measure and improve decision-making
- Separate skill from variance

### 2. Continuous Learning
**Every outcome contains information.**
- Extract lessons from both wins and losses
- Update models based on new evidence
- Stay humble about knowledge limits

### 3. Adaptive Strategy
**Be flexible as conditions change.**
- Monitor for structural shifts
- Adjust strategies as markets evolve
- Maintain optionality where possible

### 4. Competitive Advantage
**Sustain edge through superior execution.**
- Develop unique capabilities
- Protect intellectual advantages
- Continuously improve processes

### 5. Risk Awareness
**Understand and manage downside.**
- Know your absorbing barriers
- Size positions appropriately
- Maintain survival capacity
