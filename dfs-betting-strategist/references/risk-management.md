# Risk Management

## Bankroll Management and the Kelly Criterion

**Bankroll Management** is the mathematical discipline of determining optimal bet sizing. The **Kelly Criterion** provides the mathematical formula for optimal bet sizing in uncertain environments, balancing growth maximization with risk of ruin.

## The Kelly Criterion

### Mathematical Formula
```
f* = (bp - q) / b
```

Where:
- **f*** = Percentage of bankroll to bet
- **b** = Odds received (e.g., 2 to 1)
- **p** = Probability of winning
- **q** = Probability of losing (1 - p)

### Practical Application
**Example**: 60% chance to win, 2:1 odds
- f* = (2 × 0.6 - 0.4) / 2 = 0.4 = 40% of bankroll

## The Risk of Ruin

### Fundamental Principle
The Kelly Criterion proves that **over-betting your edge leads to inevitable ruin**. Even if you have a 99% probability of winning, if you bet 100% of your bankroll, the 1% failure eventually occurs, and your capital goes to zero (an **absorbing barrier**).

### The Absorption Barrier Concept
- **Definition**: A state from which you cannot recover
- **Examples**: Bankruptcy, career-ending injury, business failure
- **Prevention**: Never bet more than you can afford to lose

### Startup Parallel
Startups often fail not because the idea was bad (Positive EV), but because they:
1. **Burned cash too fast** (Over-betting Kelly)
2. **Hit a variance streak** (Market downturn)
3. **Ran out of runway** before their edge could realize

**"Runway" is simply a function of proper Kelly sizing.**

## Via Negativa and Negative Selection

### Risk Management Philosophy
Risk management is often about what **not** to do (**Via Negativa**). Often, the highest ROI comes from eliminating negative activities rather than adding positive ones.

### Negative Selection Framework

#### Biological Analogy
In biology, the immune system uses **negative selection** to eliminate T-cells that attack the self. In engineering, negative selection algorithms detect anomalies by defining what is "not self."

#### Strategic Application
Instead of trying to be brilliant, focus on **not being stupid**:
- **Eliminate negative-EV habits**: Smoking, poor financial management, toxic relationships
- **Remove risk amplifiers**: Excessive leverage, concentrated positions
- **Avoid absorbing barriers**: Don't bet more than you can afford to lose

### ROI Comparison
- **Adding positive habits**: Moderate improvement, moderate cost
- **Removing negative habits**: Often higher ROI, lower cost
- **Examples**: 
  - Stop checking phone during focus time vs. trying new productivity apps
  - Eliminate high-fee investment products vs. finding better investments
  - Remove toxic relationships vs. making new friends

## Practical Risk Management Frameworks

### Position Sizing Rules

#### The 2% Rule
Never risk more than 2% of total bankroll on any single bet/investment.

#### Kelly Fraction Adjustment
- **Full Kelly**: Maximum theoretical growth
- **Half Kelly**: Reduce volatility, still strong growth
- **Quarter Kelly**: Conservative approach, minimal drawdowns

#### Dynamic Sizing
- **Increase size** with growing bankroll
- **Decrease size** during drawdown periods
- **Pause** after significant losses to reassess

### Portfolio-Level Risk Management

#### Diversification
- **Asset allocation**: Don't put all eggs in one basket
- **Correlation consideration**: Avoid highly correlated positions
- **Rebalancing**: Take profits from winners, add to losers

#### Stop-Loss Mechanisms
- **Hard stops**: Predefined exit points
- **Soft stops**: Monitor and adjust based on conditions
- **Volatility stops**: Exit based on price movements

## Applications Across Domains

### Personal Finance

#### Emergency Fund
- **Target**: 6-12 months of expenses
- **Purpose**: Absorb unexpected shocks
- **Location**: High liquidity, low risk

#### Insurance Strategy
- **High probability, low impact**: Self-insure
- **Low probability, high impact**: Transfer risk through insurance
- **Goal**: Avoid absorbing barriers

### Business Strategy

#### Cash Flow Management
- **Operating cash flow**: Monitor monthly
- **Burn rate**: Track against runway
- **Contingency planning**: Prepare for downside scenarios

#### Investment Decisions
- **Due diligence**: Understand downside before upside
- **Scenario planning**: Model best/worst/likely cases
- **Exit strategies**: Know when to cut losses

### Career Development

#### Skill Diversification
- **Multiple revenue streams**: Reduce single-point failure
- **Network building**: Create opportunity pipeline
- **Continuous learning**: Stay relevant in changing markets

#### Risk Assessment
- **Job security**: Understand your replaceability
- **Industry trends**: Monitor for disruption
- **Geographic flexibility**: Don't get trapped in declining regions

## Behavioral Risk Management

### Cognitive Bias Control

#### Overconfidence Bias
- **Problem**: Believing you can beat the odds consistently
- **Solution**: Conservative position sizing, regular self-assessment

#### Loss Aversion
- **Problem**: Holding losers too long, selling winners too early
- **Solution**: Predefined exit criteria, mechanical decision-making

#### Recency Bias
- **Problem**: Overweighting recent experiences
- **Solution**: Long-term historical analysis, process focus

### Decision-Making Frameworks

#### Pre-Mortem Analysis
- **Question**: "This decision failed. Why?"
- **Benefits**: Identify hidden risks before they materialize
- **Application**: Strategic planning, major investments

#### Devil's Advocate
- **Process**: Challenge your own assumptions
- **Benefits**: Reduce confirmation bias
- **Method**: Force yourself to argue the opposite position

## Advanced Risk Concepts

### Tail Risk Hedging
- **Black Swan protection**: Small cost, massive payoff when needed
- **Examples**: Put options, diversification, insurance
- **Philosophy**: Pay small amounts to avoid ruin

### Stress Testing
- **Scenario analysis**: Model extreme but possible events
- **Historical stress**: How did portfolio perform in past crises?
- **Forward testing**: How would current positions handle crisis?

### Correlation Risk
- **Hidden connections**: When "diversified" positions move together
- **Crisis correlation**: All correlations go to 1 during panic
- **Solution**: Stress test correlations, don't assume they hold

---

*Risk management is not about avoiding all risks—it's about taking the right risks in the right sizes. Survival is the first rule of any probabilistic game.*
